101

Financial
Fluency

"The CEO does not care about your burn-down chart. They care about the P&L."

[Theory]

Stop Showing Roadmaps, Show P&L ↗

Most PMs manage velocity. You must manage Financial Impact. Learn to track "Revenue Protected" and "Cost Savings" instead of story points.

[Framework]

CapEx vs. OpEx Strategy ↗

Treating your engineering team as an investment portfolio. Are you allocating 60% to Growth (CapEx) or 80% to Maintenance (OpEx)?

[Tactic]

How to Read a P&L ↗

Revenue Quality (ARR vs Service), COGS Efficiency (Hosting/API costs), and OpEx ROI (R&D Spend).

[Concept]

The Senior PM Trap ↗

Why high-performers hit a ceiling. You are technically literate but financially illiterate. Break the ceiling by mastering Unit Economics.

201

AI Unit
Economics

"If you can't monetize AI, you are just paying for compute."

[Diagnostic]

The Financial Leadership Test ↗

AI isn't a tech problem. It's a P&L test. Focus on Automation ROI: Identifying internal tasks to automate for immediate efficiency.

[Strategy]

The $25M Strategic Lever ↗

Stop building chatbots. Start solving the "Crisis of Context." Use AI to automate strategic performance tracking for the C-Suite.

[Warning]

The API Gold Mine Trap ↗

You aren't buying a service; you're paying to be disrupted. How to weaponize your data moat against OpenAI.

Executive
Rigor

"Innovation without operation is hallucination."

[Mindset]

The "Busy" Trap ↗

If your team is exhausted, your leadership is the weak link. Become a Master of Clarity.

[Alignment]

Cross-Department Alignment ↗

Resolve silos with "Radical Transparency." Create a unified North Star that ties Engineering velocity to Business outcomes.

[Tactics]

The One Decision ↗

In 0-to-1 environments, ambiguity is the enemy. Define a direction so clear the team wants to rally, even if the data isn't perfect yet.

[Culture]

Leading Under Pressure ↗

The "Quarterback" Mindset. Staying composed when the system fails is the difference between a glitch and a crisis.
