The Career Arc

I did not start as a theorist. I am an operator who has fixed broken machines at every level of the enterprise.

THE OPERATING THESIS "You cannot govern a roadmap if you do not own the budget. Innovation without operation is hallucination."

Phase 1: The Operator (Director of Operations)

Before I led Product, I led P&Ls. As a Director of Operations and Chief of Staff for a mid-market firm, I didn't just ship features—I owned the bottom line. I inherited a stagnant P&L and fractured silos. By instituting operational rigor and consolidating disjointed teams, I drove 200% year-over-year growth to $20M.

This period taught me that "efficiency" isn't about cutting costs; it's about killing the activities that don't compound capital.

Phase 2: The Scaler (Director of Product)

I transitioned to product leadership with a mandate to fix "broken velocity." At a major enterprise firm, I inherited a paralyzing 200-item backlog and a team shipping nothing. I installed a "Quantified Prioritization System" that doubled output and slashed a $5M operational cost via a complex Azure cloud migration.

In the public sector, I managed critical infrastructure for 7.7 million users, delivering a massive technology overhaul with 98% on-time delivery in a zero-fail environment.

Phase 3: The Board Advisor (Product Economist)

Today, I serve on Advisory Boards and function as a Fractional Executive. I bridged the gap between code and capital for "Entrepreneurs of Tomorrow" and act as a Contributing Expert for Foundry.

I created the Office of the Product Economist because I realized that as AI scales, the "Innovation Tax" is becoming a solvency risk. Boards needed a new language to manage probabilistic cost.
